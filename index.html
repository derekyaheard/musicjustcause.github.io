DYHR – MusicJustCause – Piano Lessons Overview This page outlines the range of instructional formats available for students interested in piano, music theory, ear training, and creative musical development. Each format is adaptable to the student’s goals, experience level, and preferred pace. Instructional Formats 1. Exploratory Q&A Sessions • Focus — Open conversation about technique, theory, repertoire, improvisation, or practice strategy. • Structure — Student-led questions guide the session; instructor provides demonstrations, explanations, and conceptual framing. • Ideal for — Curious learners, self-taught musicians, returning adults, and those seeking clarity on specific musical ideas. 2. Beginner Piano Lessons • Focus — Foundational technique, reading, rhythm, ear development, and musical confidence. • Structure — Step-by-step progression with clear goals and supportive guidance. • Ideal for — New learners of any age. 3. Intermediate Piano Lessons • Focus — Expanding technique, expressive playing, repertoire building, and stylistic understanding. • Structure — Personalized curriculum based on the student’s strengths and interests. • Ideal for — Students with prior experience seeking structured growth. 4. Music Theory Instruction • Focus — Fundamentals such as intervals, scales, harmony, voice leading, form, and analysis. • Structure — Concept-driven lessons with examples at the piano. • Ideal for — Students preparing for exams, composers, producers, and theory-curious learners. 5. Ear Training and Listening Development • Focus — Interval recognition, chord quality identification, melodic dictation, and tonal awareness. • Structure — Progressive exercises supported by real-time demonstration. • Ideal for — Musicians seeking stronger internalization of pitch and harmony. 6. Improvisation and Creative Practice • Focus — Building fluency, exploring motifs, understanding harmonic environments, and developing personal style. • Structure — Guided improvisation, listening analysis, and creative prompts. • Ideal for — Pianists, producers, and composers. 7. Customized Hybrid Sessions • Focus — A blend of technique, theory, ear training, repertoire, and creative exploration. • Structure — Tailored to the student’s goals, whether short-term or long-term. • Ideal for — Learners seeking a flexible, evolving curriculum. Teaching Approach • Emphasis on clarity, musical understanding, and practical application. • Supportive, patient, and adaptable instruction. • Lessons designed to cultivate independence, curiosity, and expressive confidence. Getting Started Students may begin with a single exploratory session or choose a structured path. All formats can be adjusted as the student’s interests evolve.
